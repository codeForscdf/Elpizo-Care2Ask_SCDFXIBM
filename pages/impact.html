Current Statistics

Before we set out on attacking the problem statement--"Emergency Medical Services" with hardcore coding, let us look aat the statistics we are going against to help us be mindful of what we are up against.

  • 1 in 10 elderly Singaporeans above the age of 60 has dementia. This figure of 48,000 only includes dementia patients(figures from IMH 2018), , and does not count other forms of mental and physical disability. We will see the implications in years to come.
  • 187,607 calls is the total number of calls SCDF received in the year 2019, 43.8% of these calls come from/for the elderly above 60, and the numbers are increasing yearly. (from SCDF.gov.sg) (figures from SingStats 2018)
  • by 2040, 30 percent of Singapore's population will be above the age of 60.
  • Of the 46,689 registered responders only 4,800 of them are active users regularly opening the application. (2019 stats from SCDF.gov.sg)
  • Chute fires, CPR and choking myResponder app currently has instructions for these cases. Every year, the number of calls they receive are increasing but the number of people joining the force from National Service is decreasing.
