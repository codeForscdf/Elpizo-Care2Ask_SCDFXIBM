Welcome to Team Elpizo's webpage. As part of the SCDFxIBM Innovation Challenge 2020, we will discuss the everchanging problem Singapore faces in the area of emergency response.

Recent advances in cloud technologies have created opportunities to ease the operations of many sectors. Are these cloud technologies just a replacement for FTPs and VMs? Or can it's wide arrays of APIs and SDKs be used too in managing Singapore's pursuit to be a smart nation?

The Problem Statements

  • EMERGENCY MEDICAL SERVICES

    SCDF works closely with Community First Responders (CFRs) to provide timely relief and response to emergency situations. With the increasingly aging population and a growing segment of vulnerable populations in mind (e.g. increasing trend of elderly with no next of kin), how might we leverage analytics for better sense-making to be alerted at the onset of incidents which require emergency response (e.g. cardiac arrests, falls, unattended cooking fires etc.) and mobilise CFRs for effective early intervention especially to the vulnerable populations?

  • CLIMATE CHANGE

    Climate change is inevitable, with projected increase in temperatures leading to phenomena such as the Urban Heat Island effect. This leads to an environment and climate where it is increasingly physically challenging for First Responders to train and operate to maximum efficiency and performance. How might SCDF leverage wearables or other technologies to provide relief or enhancement in harsh operating conditions and maximise the safety, health and performance of First Responders during training and operations?

  • INTERGRATING WITH A SMART ENVIRONMENT

    Infrastructure is getting “smart”, with sensors and Internet of things (IoT) increasingly embedded in the built environment (e.g. Punggol Digital District). How might we leverage a network of smart infrastructure in the built environment to make better and more timely sense of emergency incidents (e.g. detection of fires developing, building collapses, falls, road traffic accidents etc.) and to trigger early intervention measures, without the need to activate precious emergency resources?

  • PREVENTING THE SPREAD

    Recent events such as the protracted wildfires in Australia and the outbreak of COVID-19 suggests that across the globe, we are indeed heading toward a decade of new frontiers in 2020s which has implications on civil defence, community resilience and emergency response in Singapore. How might we leverage technology to project and come up with upstream solutions to make such events non-events?

Resources Our Final Solution Uses

GitHub

GitHub

A great space to store code and share them in organised folders, so that our team can simultaneously work together on one project. The ease of collaboration stems from GitHub's ergonomic design. Their great integrations with many applications allow us to work seemlessly.

Atom.io

Atom.io

Atom.io is an amazing code editor where we all code our applications before compiling in an IDE and then uploading to server via FTP. Atom.io is heavily customisable based on individual preferences and has neat features to allow collaborations.

AWS Rekognition

AWS Rekognition

We use AWS Rekognition to run the comparison of faces for our Happy Sundowning application. It's high accuracy in facial detection and comparison even in pictures with low quality is a powerful tool we created for the public to help our society's pertinent issue of an ageing population.

Google Vision

Google Vision

We worked with this optical character recognition tool to extract tough words to convert with Medify, an application in our solution, convertin complex text into comprehensible text. This translation aims to help CFRs better understand patient conditions and promote transparency in the healthcare sector.

IBM Cloud

IBM Cloud

A store for many other softwares, IBM Cloud makes it easy to incorporate the many AI tools into our programmes. We worked on phpmyadmin on IBM, as well used IBM's Db2 database on their cloud before deploying to our servers. All in all, it is a host for a plethora of applications to connect together.

IBM Watson Assistant

IBM Watson Assistant

We've taught IBM's Watson Assistant SCDF's SOPs and the medical protocols, coupling with IBM's Speech to Text capabilities. This is to provide quick information to paramedics out on scene attending to cases. It is an easy application to create but has an even bigger application on the field.
